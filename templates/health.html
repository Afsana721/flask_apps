Blood Basics

Blood is a specialized body fluid. It has four main components: plasma, red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets. The blood that runs through the veins, arteries, and capillaries is known as whole bloodâ€”a mixture of about 55% plasma and 45% blood cells. About 7% to 8% of your total body weight is blood. An average-sized man has about 12 pints of blood in his body, and an average-sized woman has about 9 pints.

Plasma

The liquid component of blood is called plasma, a mixture of water, sugar, fat, protein, and salts. The main job of plasma is to transport blood cells throughout the body along with nutrients, waste products, antibodies, clotting proteins, chemical messengers (such as hormones), and proteins that help maintain the body's fluid balance.

The Human Brain: Cognitive and Neurological Architecture

Exploring the Mind's Complexities

The Seat of Consciousness

The brain is the most complex organ, governing everything from motor skills and sensory processing to **consciousness, memory, and emotional regulation**. Understanding its intricate architecture is key to advancing neuroscience and treating neurological disorders.

Neurological Development and Disease

Interactive 3D models allow researchers and students to visualize the impact of **developmental changes, trauma, and diseases** like Alzheimer's or Parkinson's on specific brain regions. This facilitates superior diagnostics and surgical planning.
